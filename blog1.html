My First Internship as a Frontend Developer

Company: GBCS Group

Duration: 3 months, remote

When I joined GBCS Group as a frontend intern, I had no idea how fast things would move. I was part of a 20-person intern team tasked with building internal platforms to support fleet optimization operations. We used React, Node.js, and Next.js—technologies I’d studied before, but now I was using them in real-world projects, with real deadlines, and real expectations.

One of my favorite parts was working closely with our project manager. I learned how to write clear documentation, manage feature rollouts, and communicate technical decisions. Over time, I took on more responsibility and eventually became the team lead. That meant coordinating tasks, mentoring other interns, and even leading our weekly meetings with the CEO.

It was intense, but incredibly rewarding. I got to see how a tech team operates from the inside, and I walked away with stronger skills, deeper confidence, and a clearer sense of how I want to grow as a developer.

This internship wasn’t just about writing code—it was about learning how to lead, collaborate, and build something that matters.
